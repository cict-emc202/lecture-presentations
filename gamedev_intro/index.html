You can start by writing down and organizing all of your ideas in the Game Design Document (GDD).

So what is exactly
a GDD?

The GDD is a living design document created by the development team as result of collaboration between their designers, artists and programmers.

It does't have a general format but the GDD may contain things like...

  • Game's story
  • Concept art
  • Gameplay mechanics
  • Assets list to build
  • Development schedule
  • Development tools

*You also need to make concept art for your characters and environments so you know how you want things to look, and to share with your team.

Game's story

Tell the story of your game. It might be simple or highly complex, but knowing your story will guide many other design decisions.

Concept art

Create concept art to visually establish and communicate the look and feel of your game, so artists can build it and give it life.

Gameplay mechanics

How will your player and your game elements interact?

Assets list

What are the stuffs that you need for your game? Put 'em on a list.

Development schedule

Put your game development tasks on schedule.

Development tools

List the software tools or frameworks that will help you bring all your characters and environments together, layout your levels, and build great gameplay.

Build

Assemble 'em up.

The build phase is where you start putting together everything you’ve created so far.

This is where you import all your game assets into the game engine, lay out levels, script gameplay, and optimize performance.

It's important to keep testing the game as you build it, to make sure it’s fun to play. Ask people you trust to test your game and give you feedback. The ultimate goal is to make a fun experience that can be shared with other players.

source: http://www.autodesk.com/campaigns/makegames/build

This is also the phase where you assemble your graphic assets and UI.
